# Visual design

The look should sell the joke before the copy does. A reader landing cold should think
"this is the official site of a real international sporting body" for at least a few seconds.

## Reference aesthetic

Real-world models to study:
- World Chess Federation (FIDE) — fide.com
- World Bridge Federation — worldbridge.org
- International Skating Union — isu.org
- World Curling Federation — worldcurling.org

Common traits to adopt:
- Conservative palette: deep green, navy, gold accents, off-white background
- Serif headlines, sans body
- Crest/logo prominent in top-left
- Top nav bar with formal sections (About, Rules, News, Events, Members)
- Footer with HQ address, "© International Cribbage Association 19XX–2026," social links
  (which can 404 or go nowhere — they're props)

## Color palette (proposed)

- Primary: deep felt green `#1f3d2b`
- Secondary: navy `#0f2540`
- Accent: muted gold `#b08a3e`
- Background: warm off-white `#f6f1e6`
- Text: near-black `#1a1a1a`

## Typography (proposed)

- Headlines: Playfair Display (serif, weighted)
- Body: Source Sans 3 or Inter
- Numerals / scores: a monospaced or tabular figure variant for the rules section

## Layout

- Max content width ~1100px, centered
- Generous whitespace, formal grid
- Hero on home page with crest, motto, and a single tagline (e.g. "Stewards of the game since 1947")
- News styled like a press-release index, not a blog

## What to avoid

- Modern startup gradients, glassmorphism, hand-drawn illustrations
- Emoji anywhere in the UI
- Casual microcopy ("Hey there!", "Let's get started")
- Animated splash, parallax scrolling, dark mode toggle — federations don't do that
